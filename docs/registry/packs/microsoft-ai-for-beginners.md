## What it is

A 12-week, 24-lesson curriculum covering AI fundamentals: neural networks, deep learning, computer vision, NLP, symbolic AI, knowledge representation, GAs, multi-agent systems, and ethics. Practical Jupyter notebooks using TensorFlow and PyTorch, plus quizzes and labs.

## When to consult

- Onboarding teams to AI broadly (not just classic ML or just LLMs).
- Projects involving computer vision, NLP, or symbolic AI.
- Teams needing AI ethics grounding.

## When NOT to consult

- Project focuses purely on classic ML — use `microsoft-ml-for-beginners` instead.
- Project focuses purely on LLMs / generative AI — use `microsoft-generative-ai-for-beginners`.

## How to use

Reference course — no install. Run notebooks locally.

## Fit signals

- Project involves CV, NLP, or symbolic AI.
- Team needs broad AI literacy beyond LLMs.

## Conflicts and overlaps

- Explicitly distinct from `microsoft-ml-for-beginners` (classic ML) and `microsoft-generative-ai-for-beginners` (genAI). Read all three for the full Microsoft beginner stack.

## Notes

Wider scope than ML-For-Beginners; includes older AI paradigms (symbolic, GAs) useful for foundational understanding but rarely used in modern SaaS.
