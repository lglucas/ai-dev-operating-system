# 2026-05-01 — v0.4.3 quick wins (ETHOS + 3 skills + agents-marketplace tag)

## Context

The v0.4.2 release (2026-04-30) shipped Registry batch 3 and bundled an in-chat side-by-side comparison study of `slavingia/skills` and `gstack` against this OS. That study identified **five quick wins** suitable for a follow-up patch release and **five architectural items** that needed to wait for v0.5.

A subsequent v0.5 audit (early 2026-05-01) confirmed that the architectural items required human-in-the-loop decisions before they could be safely sequenced — but the quick wins did not. The user explicitly approved attacking the green block (quick wins) while the red block (decisions) waits.

This entry records why each of the five quick wins shipped the way it did.

## Decision 1 — Ship `ETHOS.md` as a one-page manifesto, not as a chapter inside `CLAUDE.md`

`CLAUDE.md` is the operating contract for AI agents working in the repo: structural, prescriptive, long. `.claude/rules/golden-rules.md` is a 10-line reminder file. Neither is a manifesto: neither is short enough to read in 90 seconds AND principled enough to settle ambiguous design debates.

ETHOS.md fills the gap with 15 numbered principles. It has no enforcement role — agents still consult `CLAUDE.md` and `.claude/rules/` for behavior — but it is the appeal court when a future change cannot be defended against the rules as written. Items 13, 14, and 15 explicitly anchor the three new skills shipped in this release; the remaining 12 items distill prior versions' golden rules.

## Decision 2 — `/processize` is gated, opinionated, and explicitly refuses to automate

The temptation in vibe coding is to automate every repeating task on its first reappearance. The skill ships with a 5-question gate that **stops** if the workflow has been done fewer than 3 times, or if the last 3 attempts didn't all produce the desired outcome. The skill never builds the automation itself — it generates `docs/processes/<slug>.md` and stops; implementation is a sprint task.

The 3-stage maturity model (manual → documented → processized) was chosen over a 4- or 5-stage model because every additional stage adds vocabulary the user must remember, and existing skills already cover the implementation phase (`feature-scaffold`, `sprint-management`).

## Decision 3 — `/grow-sustainably` rejects vanity metrics by construction

The skill is opinionated: it refuses to render dashboards that show signups instead of active users, refuses to advise paid channels before `usage-monitor` is live, caps automated outreach at 50/day even after `/processize`, and gates new channels behind a 4-week plateau on the prior channel.

The four-pillar order (retention → compress → ONE new channel → founder bandwidth) is sequential, not parallel. Running them in parallel was rejected because that's how vibe coders burn out: five 20%-effort channels instead of one 100%-effort channel.

The "founder bandwidth check" pillar is the explicit guardrail against the most common silent failure: a working SaaS that fails because its founder broke first.

## Decision 4 — `/multi-ai-review` uses MIN, not mean, for net confidence

This is the single most consequential design choice in the skill. Averaging confidence across reviewers rewards consensus and punishes dissent. The opposite is true: a reviewer scoring 4 with a specific concern is more valuable than three reviewers scoring 9 with vague approval.

`Net confidence = min(individual scores)` makes the dissenter the gatekeeper. To proceed, every reviewer must reach ≥7. To unblock a 4, the artifact must change — not the review process.

A second design choice: at least one reviewer must have a different lens (different agent role OR different model family). Same-model self-review is weaker than cross-model review; the skill explicitly forbids it.

## Decision 5 — `#agents-marketplace` is a new tag, not a section inside `#ecosystem`

The v0.4.2 study revealed that ~11 of the 57 packs aren't single-purpose tools — they're bundled marketplaces with their own internal structure (gstack's 23 agents, slavingia's 10 skills, antigravity's 1,441 playbooks, etc.). Folding them under `#ecosystem` was rejected because:

- `#ecosystem` is for broad expansions (often community-driven and exploratory).
- `#foundations` is for canonical references (Anthropic and similarly authoritative).
- The marketplace bundle pattern needed its own surface — same goes for the install conflict-resolution playbook, which doesn't fit either of the two existing tags.

The tag also captures the new install rule: when adopting any marketplace bundle, run `/registry-pick` and `/multi-ai-review` first.
